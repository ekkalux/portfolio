MOBILE APPLICATION | BLOC PROJECT | CASE STUDY

Today, defining and maintaining fitness is open to interpretation. Whether that be health, sports, or recreational activity, Gymseeker seeks to assist users in finding the perfect gym to achieving their fitness goals.

Problem Statement

There are too many gyms to choose from

Finding the right gym is different for everyone. Whether that is location, price or types of services offered, users must go through a rigorous research process before committing. Applications such as Yelp does aid in the process of locating a gym, but it isn't comprehensive enough to cover the entirety of a gym's profile. Additionally, when expectations aren't met, users are not satisfied with their gym and may potentially neglect their fitness goals.

Solution Overview

Achieving Fitness Goals at the Right Gym

Gymseeker makes the process of finding a gym easier by streamlining the research and selection phase based on the users' needs. Users can search for gyms based on location and view gym's profile before making a commitment to purchasing a membership, easing users in the decision-making process.

Discovery & Strategy Phase

Getting to know my Users

I initiated my research by crafting a 15 questions user survey to find out users' goals and pain points. By targeting audiences from all experiences with gym-related components, I gathered more than 40 comprehensive responses. From this survey, I needed to learn the following:

  • User's fitness goals
  • What factor is essential in selecting a gym
  • What frustrations do users have with their current gym
  • What stops users from going to the gym
  • What type of membership do they currently have

Interviewing my Users

Research Insights

1. Buying a gym membership can be stressful for most users. With all the different factors presented (e.g community, overcrowding, lack of certain types of equipments), it is vital that Gymseeker eases users into simplifying decision-making when choosing a gym.

2. People want to meet their fitness goals, but find it hard to do so when they are met with pain points like being too tired from work or the gym is too far to make the trip. They will make more of the effort if their external inhibitors aren't deterring them.

3. I learned that users tend to associate gyms with the weight room. Based on this insight, I want to ensure that a gym profile on screen contains weight-related elements.

Who are my Audiences

To gain a better understanding of what specific features were desired and what kind of people are likely to user Gymseeker, I compiled my research to create three different user personas: Avid gym-goer, occasional gym-goer, and amateur gym-goer. These personas will guide me to design an intuitive flow for Gymseeker.

Usability Phase

Iterating the Button Sizes

When users were tasked with finding a gym, I noticed that users tend to stay on the map view and didn't see the list view unless otherwise hinted. Besides, designing mobile applications require buttons to be more prominent due to users' thumb size. By making the accessibility for both views larger, users can easily see and navigate from one perspective to the other.

Designing the List View

When presented with the list view, users mentioned that they had trouble reading some of the contents. To accommodate this pain point, I increased readability by increasing the fonts universally and applying a darker visual element to the list view. Showing this screen again, users were impressed with the visual hierarchy of the screen.

Reflections

1. Gymseeker is a project that I am particularly passionate about. As a fitness enthusiast who frequents the weight room, I have only considered locating gyms based on what types of equipment a gym has in their weight room. With Gymseeker, users are presented with a holistic detail of a gym's profile before committing to purchasing a membership.

2. For this iOS application, I delved deep into the user testing process and have made many iterations based on feedback. In future development of the current product and due to time constraints, I would consider an additional feature that allows users to sort through the different types of a gym - Yoga center, dance center, athletic clubs, etc. This would provide users with a comprehensive direction to filter through their fitness goals.
